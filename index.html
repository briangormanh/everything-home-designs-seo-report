Why the review gap still matters

Reviews and links both build authority — the same lever that lets the client pull ahead. And reviews are a trust signal a real person sees: a homeowner comparing the map pack notices 68 next to Chateau's 280 and may hesitate, even when the client outranks them. Close the Google-review gap first; third-party platforms (Houzz, Yelp, Angi) are secondary — and Houzz (122+ 5-star, top 1%) is already a real strength.

Local pages, Carmel overlap, and geographic breadth

Three separate things often get lumped together here. Only the first is true "cannibalization" — multiple pages competing for the same search. The other two are content-quality and positioning issues, not cannibalization.

1 · A real but narrow overlap — the general Carmel pages

/carmel (positioned as "Carmel Interior Designers and Remodelers"), /carmel-home-remodeling, /carmel-interior-designers, and the homepage all target the same general "Carmel remodeling / interior design" search — so they genuinely compete. (The service-specific Carmel pages — kitchen, bath, basement — are fine: different services, different searches.) Fix: give each page a distinct job, or fold the catch-all /carmel page into the dedicated ones.

2 · The city pages are templated, not competing

Fishers, Zionsville, Westfield, Noblesville, McCordsville, and Geist each own their own city, so they don't compete with each other. The issue is they're near-identical — the same copy with the city name swapped — which is thin and under-differentiated, and that "same template across many cities" pattern is what Google's quality scrutiny targets (and what past agencies left behind). Fix: make each page genuinely local — real local projects, neighborhoods, specifics — or prune the cities there's no business case for. Keep separate pages for separate cities; don't merge them.

3 · The site reads as "Carmel-only"

Because the core service pages are all Carmel-named, the site misses the wider metro. Worth broadening some to a metro / Indiana focus so they can rank beyond Carmel. (Also: /indiana-basement-design-remodeling is thin (335 words) and barely linked — merge it into /carmel-basement-remodeler.)

Moderate confidence  The Carmel overlap is read from the pages' titles and headers — the crawl's duplicate-detection wasn't turned on. Re-crawl with it on to confirm exactly how much those general Carmel pages overlap.

Conversion options & CTAs

There are a few ways to convert on the site, and the placement shows which one the business is betting on. Each could work harder.

  • Phone — the primary path. It sits in the header, so calls are clearly the main conversion. Make it pull its weight: larger, in a standout color (or a button/icon treatment), click-to-call on mobile, and repeated inside the section CTAs — not just the header.
  • Contact form — the secondary path. On the homepage it's buried at the very bottom. Bring it up to mid-page (it doesn't need to be above the fold) and frame it as the alternative — e.g. "Prefer to write? Tell us about your project." Keep the fields minimal and confirm it submits to a thank-you page.
  • "Schedule a Consultation" — the offer. Today it lives only in the nav and footer. Add a high-contrast button in the hero of every money page, with consistent copy through the flow.
  • Worth testing for a $40k–$100k+ buyer: a text option or a booking link as a low-friction secondary — only if it fits how they actually field leads.

Verify live  Phone and form placements are read from static screenshots — confirm them on the live site and in the mobile view, and check the form submits to a working thank-you page.
